About the Reich Lab

Based in the Department of Biostatistics and Epidemiology at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, the Reich Lab focuses on developing statistical methods and tools for data arising from infectious disease settings.

We use statistics, data science, and epidemiology to gain better understanding of the complexities of infectious disease dynamics. Our work has been featured in the New York Times and on New England Public Radio.

With active funded projects from the NIH, CDC, and VA, the Lab is involved in independent and collaborative research efforts. Our collaborators include the Infectious Disease Dynamics Working Group at Johns Hopkins, the Thai Ministry of Public Health, the Dengue Branch of the CDC in Puerto Rico, the Children's Hospital Colorado, and the Veterans Health Administration branches in New York City and Iowa City.
